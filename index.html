Overview

The “Arohanagara” project is our attempt to understand how data structures and algorithms can support the design of a modern city. Instead of looking at DAA only as a theoretical subject, we wanted to explore how concepts like graphs, shortest path algorithms, trees, and optimization techniques connect to real-world planning.

Throughout this course, we studied how different data structures evolve based on the kind of problems they solve. This helped us recognize that city planning is, in many ways, a large-scale data problem — roads behave like weighted graphs, zoning can be represented in hierarchical structures, and resource management often relies on efficient searching and balancing algorithms.

Our objective was not only to design a hypothetical smart city but to present a structured model that shows how algorithmic thinking improves decision-making. Whether it is deciding the best route between zones, distributing utilities, or estimating budgets, each part of the city benefits from a well-chosen algorithm. Arohanagara is therefore a demonstration of how classroom concepts can be meaningfully connected to real planning challenges.
